Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol (TCP/IP)

The internet's "Terms and Conditions."

TCP/IP is the rulebook that makes sure computers don't get confused. TCP is the micromanager who breaks your data into tiny boxes and counts them to make sure nothing is lost. IP is the mailman who puts the address on the box. Together, they are the reason your email doesn't accidentally end up on your neighbor's smart fridge.
